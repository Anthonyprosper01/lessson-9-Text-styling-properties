Boxing is a combat sport in which two athletes, called boxers, engage in a contest of strength, skill, and strategy by fighting each other using their fists, typically while wearing gloves. It is contested in a series of timed rounds, and the goal is to score points by landing legal punches on the opponent or, in some cases, to knock the opponent out (KO) or make them unable to continue the fight (technical knockout or TKO). Boxing matches take place in a square ring, and competitors are categorized by weight classes, from flyweight (up to 112 lbs) to heavyweight (over 200 lbs).
